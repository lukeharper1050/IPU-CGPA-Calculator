What is VIT CGPA Calculator?

CGPA stands for Cumulative Grade Point Average—a standardized measure used by VIT University to evaluate student performance across all semesters. It reflects your overall academic achievement by combining grades from every course you've completed, weighted by their respective credit hours.

VIT uses the CGPA system to maintain consistency and fairness in assessing students. Unlike percentage-based systems, CGPA considers both the difficulty (credits) and your performance (grade points) in each subject, giving a more balanced view of your abilities.

While you can calculate CGPA manually, the process involves multiple steps—multiplying credits with grade points, summing them up, and dividing by total credits. That's where the VIT CGPA Calculator comes in. It automates the entire process, eliminating human error and saving you valuable time.

How to Calculate CGPA in VIT University

Understanding the CGPA formula is essential for every VIT student. Here's the mathematical foundation:

CGPA = (Σ Credit × Grade Point) ÷ (Σ Credits)

  • Σ (Sigma) means "sum of"
  • Credit = number of credit hours assigned to each course
  • Grade Point = numerical value assigned to your letter grade
  • Σ Credits = total credit hours of all courses combined

Step-by-Step Calculation Guide

  1. List All Your Subjects: Create a list of all courses you've completed, with their credit hours and grades.
  2. Multiply Credits by Grade Points: For each subject, multiply credits × grade points.
  3. Add All Results: Sum the values from step 2 to get total weighted grade points.
  4. Divide by Total Credits: Divide total weighted grade points by sum of all credits to get CGPA.

How to Convert VIT CGPA to Percentage

VIT follows a specific conversion formula:

Percentage = (CGPA – 0.5) × 10

Example: CGPA = 8.6 → (8.6 – 0.5) × 10 = 81 %

  • CGPA 9.5 = 95 %
  • CGPA 9.0 = 85 %
  • CGPA 8.0 = 75 %
  • CGPA 7.5 = 70 %
  • CGPA 7.0 = 65 %

Difference Between CGPA and GPA in VIT

CGPA represents overall performance; SGPA covers a single semester. GPA is a general term often used interchangeably with SGPA in VIT.

Your CGPA is a weighted average of all SGPAs. If you score higher SGPA in a new semester, your CGPA will increase proportionally.
